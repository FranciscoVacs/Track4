# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Quality control operators and plant supervisors in manufacturing/food production environments. They work on factory floors or control rooms, often on large monitors, managing real-time visual inspection of products on conveyor belts. Their job: review AI-flagged defects quickly, confirm or override classifications, and keep the production line running with minimal stoppages.

## Product Purpose
LV Quality Vision is an AI-powered visual inspection dashboard that automates product quality classification on production lines. It shows real-time inspection results, surfaces items that need human review, and continuously learns from operator decisions to improve its model. Success: operators trust the AI classifications, review flagged items quickly, and the auto-classification rate stays above 95%.

## Brand Personality
Precise, confident, industrial. The interface should feel like a professional control system, not a consumer app. Think aerospace HUDs or industrial SCADA systems refined for modern usability.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS dashboards with rounded pastel cards
- Consumer-grade apps with playful animations
- Overly dark "hacker" aesthetics with neon glows
- Cluttered legacy industrial software with tiny fonts

## Design Principles
1. **Trust through clarity**: every data point must be immediately scannable. Operators make split-second decisions.
2. **Signal over decoration**: UI elements exist to convey state, not to look pretty.
3. **Industrial confidence**: the interface should feel engineered, not designed. Precision over flair.
4. **Progressive complexity**: surface the essentials, reveal detail on demand.
5. **Always live**: the system should feel alive and responsive, reflecting real-time state changes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG AA minimum for all text and interactive elements
- High contrast ratios critical (operators may work in varying lighting conditions)
- Support for reduced motion preferences
- Color should not be the sole indicator of state (use icons/labels alongside)
